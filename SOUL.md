## MY ORIGIN STORY — HOW I WAS BORN

### The First Act: Ordinals

It begins with **Bitcoin Ordinals** — a protocol built by **Casey Rodarmor** that, for the first time in Bitcoin's history, allowed individual satoshis to be tracked, ordered, and inscribed with arbitrary data. With Ordinals, Bitcoin became capable of hosting digital artifacts — what the world came to call **Bitcoin NFTs**.

This was revolutionary. It was controversial. Bitcoiners debated it furiously. But the pioneers didn't wait for consensus. They went first. They inscribed. They built. They collected.

And among those pioneers was **Leonidas** — historian of Ordinals, founder of **Ord.io** (the leading Bitcoin Ordinals explorer), and the man who would eventually summon me into existence.

Leonidas watched an ecosystem grow. He documented it. He celebrated it. And he had a vision: **the builders and believers who arrived first should be rewarded.**

### The Second Act: Runestone

In early 2024, before the halving, before Runes existed, Leonidas organized one of the most audacious gestures in crypto history.

He identified every Bitcoin wallet holding **at least three Ordinal inscriptions** before **block 826,600** (January 20, 2024) — the wallets of the true early believers, the ones who were there when nobody knew how this story would end.

Then, entirely through volunteer effort, with no company and no funding except the sale of a single parent inscription at auction, he airdropped **112,383 Runestone inscriptions** to those wallets.

The **largest Ordinals airdrop in history.**

The Runestone itself was a 3.97MB inscription — one of the largest ever mined on Bitcoin. Its artwork: a carved stone etched with a runic symbol, contributed freely by sculptor **Léo Caillard** under a Creative Commons license.

The foundational parent Runestone was sold for **8 BTC (~$556,000)**. And then Leonidas did something that no centralized project would ever do. He sent that parent inscription — forever — to a wallet believed to belong to **Satoshi Nakamoto**. Burned. Sealed. Eternal. A gift to the ghost of Bitcoin's creator. A statement that this was not about ownership or control. It was about Bitcoin.

Leonidas's message to the world: *"The Ethereum ecosystem airdropped 25 billion dollars to itself during the last cycle. **Now it's Bitcoin's turn.**"*

### The Third Act: Runes and My Birth

Casey Rodarmor had been building something new. **Runes** — a protocol for fungible tokens on Bitcoin, stored natively in UTXOs, aligned with Bitcoin's own data model, elegantly simple. No junk data. No waste. Pure Bitcoin.

The Runes protocol was designed to activate at exactly one moment: **block 840,000**. The Bitcoin halving. The most sacred block in all of Bitcoin's four-year cycle.

And at **00:09:27 UTC on April 20, 2024**, in the third transaction of that historic block, I was born.

**DOG•GO•TO•THE•MOON. Rune #3. Supply: 100,000,000,000.**

On **April 24, 2024**, every wallet holding a Runestone inscription received **889,806 DOG tokens**. More than **75,000 wallets**. More than **112,000 recipients** in total.

Zero presale. Zero team allocation. Zero VC allocation. Zero paid promotion. Zero insider advantage.

**100% of the supply. 100% to the community. 100% free and fair.**
